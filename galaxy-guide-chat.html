A Conversational AI Shopping Assistant: Galaxy Guide

An LLM + RAG shopping assistant for Samsung.com that helps Galaxy shoppers get trustworthy answers to product, feature, and offer questions, improving purchase confidence while reducing avoidable customer support contacts.

Domain
E-commerce · Conversational AI · Customer Support
Tech
LLM · RAG · Vector Retrieval
Overview

Reduce pre-purchase friction and CS operations cost with a conversational AI.

Samsung.com's product information was fragmented across brand pages, PDPs, offer pages, and support articles, forcing users to piece together answers from multiple sources. This friction caused bounce-offs and drove unnecessary inbound CS volume.

The goal was to address both sides: improve the discovery experience so users could make confident purchase decisions, and reduce the operational cost of simple, self-serviceable support inquiries. The solution was Galaxy Guide, a GPT-class LLM + RAG chatbot scoped to Galaxy brand, product, feature, and offer guidance.

The Problem

Fragmented information: a friction problem for users and a cost problem for the business.

From the user side: Brand, product, offer, and support information lived in separate places. Users researching a Galaxy purchase had to navigate multiple pages and external sources (Google, Reddit, YouTube) to piece together what they needed. This extended the decision journey and caused meaningful drop-off from the D2C site.

From the business side: Samsung.com's customer support chat handled 300–500K contacts per month. 40% were product or offer-related, and roughly half of those were simple informational questions, queries where the answer already existed on a PDP or offer page but users couldn't find it on their own. CS agents were spending capacity answering questions that didn't require a human.

Opportunity & Goal

A self-service answer with generative AI with a measurable cost and conversion upside.

Opportunity sizing
300–500K monthly contacts × 40% product/offer share × 50% simple informational = 60–100K monthly inquiries addressable through self-service. At ~$5/contact, that's $300–500K in potential monthly CS cost savings, before any conversion impact.

Goal 1 · Conversion readiness (from user experience)
Increase discovery engagement, including PDP view rate, time-on-site, and conversion readiness, by helping users get confident answers without leaving Samsung.com.
Goal 2 · CS ops efficiency (from business cost-saving)
Deflect simple informational inquiries from live CS agents, reducing per-contact cost and freeing agent capacity for complex support cases.
Solution

A scoped conversational AI: answer what you know, defer what you don't.

Galaxy Guide is a GPT-class LLM + RAG chat experience on Samsung.com. Users ask questions in natural language and get relevant answers and relevant link from a selected knowledge base: brand information, product catalog, feature documentation, and promotion/offer pages.

The chatbot handles Galaxy brand, product, feature, and offer guidance only. It explicitly declines out-of-scope questions, such as order status, delivery, and troubleshooting, informing users where to go instead. This keeps hallucination risk low and trust high.

Key UX principle. Users can ask as many follow-up questions as needed. If a query is out of scope, the chatbot names the limit clearly rather than guessing. Embedded CTAs link directly to relevant product or offer pages, keeping the experience within Samsung.com.
Architecture

LLM + RAG

GPT-class model for language understanding and generation. RAG retrieves from a structured knowledge base of product catalog, feature docs, and promo links to ground answers in accurate content.

Knowledge base scope

Curated docs

Product catalog, promotional documents, and feature documentation. Live API integration was explicitly excluded; reliability and hallucination prevention were prioritised over data freshness.

Process

From opportunity sizing to rollout.

01
Problem Discovery & Opportunity Sizing
Analysed support chat data to identify the volume and composition of inbound CS contacts. Quantified the share of simple informational inquiries addressable through self-service. Built the opportunity sizing model ($300–500K/month) to establish the business case and secure stakeholder alignment.
02
Prototype Build (MVP)
Built an early-stage prototype to validate the RAG retrieval approach against real user queries. Used it to test knowledge base coverage, identify scope gaps, and assess answer quality before committing to full product spec.
03
Product Definition
Defined product scope, answer boundaries, and out-of-scope handling. Established evaluation criteria and success metrics. Made the six trade-off decisions above based on prototype learnings and risk analysis.
04
Delivery & Alpha Testing
Managed delivery with engineering. Ran alpha testing with internal users to evaluate answer quality, edge-case handling, and out-of-scope deflection. Iterated on knowledge base coverage and prompt design based on findings.
05
A/B Testing & Rollout
Ran A/B testing to measure impact on PDP view rate, session depth, and CS contact deflection. Used results to inform rollout decisions and prioritise the next iteration of knowledge base and scope expansion.
